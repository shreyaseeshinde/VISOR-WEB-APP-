About VISOR

How to use this AI-Powered Guiding Shield for Vision

Getting started

1) Click Start Camera and allow camera (and mic if you plan to ask by voice).
2) You will see detections, a caption, and optional VQA answer on the right.
3) Turn on Speak captions to hear a spoken scene line (no object enumeration). Use headphones for best results.

Asking questions (VQA)

- Toggle Enable VQA and type a question, or click Ask to speak it.
- Answers are provided by Gemini when available (≤40 words), fallback to BLIP VQA.
- Examples: "list objects in the surroundings" (enumerates detections), "describe the surroundings", "is there danger ahead?"
- Turn on Speak VQA answers to hear the answer automatically. Tips: Use Chrome on localhost/HTTPS for the most reliable voice input.

Assistant vs Guide

- Assistant mode: calm scene lines every ~5 seconds (no prefix).
- Guide me: short, frequent obstacle cues every ~1.5 seconds, prefixed by “Guidance: …”.
- When both are enabled, Assistant auto-pauses while Guide is speaking to avoid overlap.

Controls

- Interval (ms): how often a frame is sent to the backend.
- Speak captions: toggle spoken scene lines on/off.
- Enable VQA: attach the current question to each frame.
- Speak VQA answers: voice out new answers when they change.
- Assistant mode: periodic scene summaries.
- Guide me: rapid obstacle guidance using detections.

Vitals

- Connect Vitals pairs with a BLE Heart Rate device (if your watch exposes the standard 0x180D service).
- Or send Google Fit vitals from a companion app to /vitals; the app will speak a short suggestion via Gemini.
- Notify vitals every 5 min: periodic spoken suggestions; Speak vitals now: immediate summary.

Troubleshooting

- No voice input: ensure mic permission is granted and try Chrome on HTTPS/localhost.
- Hearing your own voice: use headphones; the app mutes the camera stream and pauses TTS during voice input.
- Stop not stopping: we abort in-flight requests; if it persists, refresh the page.

Model notes

The backend uses YOLO for detections, BLIP for captions, Gemini for narrative/VQA (with FLAN/BLIP fallbacks). You can switch model variants and set Gemini keys via environment variables before launching the server (see README).
